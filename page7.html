Activity 3:
How would you design an interface for a 1000 floor elevator?

            
                                      As an IT student, this question will test our creativity and our strategy in building or making this kind of elevator. It will be much simpler if will break down our strategy and planning for the elevator. Are we talking about a single elevator that can cover all the 1000 floors or a multiple elevators that will only run on specific floors like even and odd numbers just like in our school. Second question, is it a company, shopping mall, hospital or any other building? A 1000 floor is just so big and could possibly covers the whole city around. Let’s just assume that this building is a multi-national company, so if this building is a multi-national building many people would go around here, this place would be busy, and lot of people are going thru elevators to reach to their specific place.


I think a single elevator that has 1000 buttons for each floor, 2 buttons for door open and close, 2 buttons for emergencies, and a multiple of switches for the maintenance operations would not work on this one. If people would pass this elevator every day, the buttons of the elevator would be likely to worn out just like the buttons in our school. Button light would likely to be busted because a lot of people are pressing the buttons every single day. Also, a 1000 floor elevator would have 1000 buttons, so there will be 1 to 1000 buttons to press with a single person. This would be so hard for the person if he/she is in a hurry. How about people who are mistakenly press the wrong and they must wait for the elevator to stop, this will be a much bigger issue when the button grid is more than 10 times of its normal size.
